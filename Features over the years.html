Over time, Stellaris has been updated small changes such as bug fixes and quality of life improvements to huge changes such as complete system reworks and lag reducing fixes.



In the early days of Stellaris, the game suffered from lag.

lag is the delay between pressing button and action happening, it is also very annoying for players. This lag was generated from various things in the game but mainly ships. Since combat in Stellaris was revolved around fleets containing army's of spaceships to fight the opposing side in the war. This meant that the empires in the game (what a computer or player plays as in stellaris) would build lots and lots of ships. By itself this is not that bad, but since the game lets you customise the weapons on your spaceships as well as different sizes of ships it means that the game needs to spend time processing all the ships, where they are, what are they doing, what type are they and what weapons do they have. This means that on low end pcs or mid to low tier laptops it cant handle this all at once meaning it reduces power from other things to help with ship calculations. This produces lag, and gamers don't like lag.

The developer's response to the gamers complaints was update 4.3 named "cetus". This update reduced lag by decreasing fleet cap (the amount of ships you can shove into a single group, called a fleet), decreasing naval cap (a game mechanic that increased ship upkeep if you went over (overly aggressive players completely ignore this mechanic)), increased the amount each ship contributed to each empires naval cap, as well as som other behind the scenes stuff. The main thing is that by making these changes the devs decreased lag over all by effectively reducing the ships in-game. Which means more happy gamers, and happy gamers don't spam messages to fix stuff.

The existence of this update greatly helps the future of stellaris as new players will enjoy less lag and not attempt to refund the game, as well as meaning people who have lower end pc's can play stellaris with out waiting one real life day per button click. Meaning the devs can get more money from sales allowing them to focus on adding more content instead of fixing performance issues.

From my experience playing with one of my favorite empires which heavily relied on ships on a medium size galaxy with 7 computer enemies. Before this update at around mid to late game it was slowing down, not because i needed to control 20 or so different planets, or my empire was at lest half the size of the galaxy, but because of my eminence out of ships, since i like to use the cheap little starter ships and build a massive swarm out of them, they was so much lag generated from the game processing my ships that the wait time from button hit to stuff happening that it was 15 to 30 seconds. Which was so painful but also the game's way of saying "don't declare more wars finnish your current three first". After this update thought playing with the same build i quickly saw how this update was not all good. Because my fleet size was drastically decreased so that mean less ships for me. Per fleet. After a bit of gaming i got to where i left of the last test and while dealing with a lot more fleets to manage than normal the drastic decrees in lag was a worthy trade off.

In the recent update 4.3 "cetus" the game changed how naval capacity worked, mainly by decreasing fleet size and changing numbers leading to less processing power needing to be used.

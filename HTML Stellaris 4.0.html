DLC added: BioGenesis, Shadows of the Shroud, Infernals.


Major changes:

Instead of the game tracking every single individual pop and job, pops are now bundled into "Pop Groups" based on their species, ethics, and strata, and they generate a "Workforce" rather than filling individual jobs one-by-one.


The 4.3 update significantly nerfed stacked economic modifiers and tech rushing, making empires less overpowered overall. Naval capacity was multiplied (battleships now cost 40 naval cap, corvettes cost 5), vastly reducing the total number of ships in play and giving specialized ships (like frigates) a clearer role.


Why:

This was introduced to drastically reduce the late-game lag that had plagued Stellaris for years.


Early versions of 4.x led to a massive power creep, allowing players and the AI to build fleets exponentially stronger than in the 3.x era. The new system makes individual ships feel more valuable and slows down the runaway snowballing of the mid-game economy.

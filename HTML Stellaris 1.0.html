Major changes:

Between Stellaris version 1.0 and 1.9, there was no massive overhaul in required system hardware (CPU/RAM/GPU), but there was a significant shift in the gameplay design—from a tile-based planet system to a more, later, pop-focused system—that eventually pushed hardware demands higher by version 2.0 and beyond.

While 64-bit was needed earlier, by update 1.9 it was more firmly established,allowing the game to utilize more than 4GB of RAM, which helped manage massive late-game simulations.


Why:

The Stellaris developers moved from the tile-based system (before update 2.2) to a pop-focused, district-based system (beginning in update 2.2, Le Guin) to fix inherent structural limitations, excessive late-game micromanagement, and poor AI performance. The original tile system limited planet growth to a fixed number of tiles (max 25), leading to small planets, and forced players to manually drag pop icons to tiles to maximize production.
